Yeng K Tan



Synopsis:


Mister Yam - a twentysomething year Malaysian man disillusioned with corporate work in San Francisco - would find his life forever changed after an inexplicable phone call with a strange woman and an invitation to a musical show.



Thus begins a series of events that would take Mister Yam chasing nameless figures across the country; solving a mystery only he can explain.



Praise for Mister Yam:


“In this debut novel…Tan’s cerebral novel is reminiscent of Haruki Murakami both in its structure and its dreamlike prose…The tale’s tone is charmingly offbeat.” — Kirkus Reviews



“It’s like the millennial’s version of a Burning Man revelations within one’s ho-hum life...There’s no one way to explain the deep message of Mister Yam, but reading it is a journey in itself; one that I highly encourage.” — Musing Mystical



“A spectacularly whimsical foray into the unknown…It is evident that Tan has relied upon a synthesis of influences to construct a narrative that is, all in all, extremely encompassing and engaging” - Luna Khalil, Five Star Goodreads Review




About the author



Yeng K Tan was born in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia. Growing up in a family of foodies, he developed an affinity for various cheeses and most salted delicacies found in a charcuterie board. He moved to California at the age of sixteen, eventually settling in San Francisco. It was in the foggy city where Tan first began working on his debut novel, Mister Yam.



Tan’s writing has been characterized as mysterious, whimsical; psychedelic even. He attributes his offbeat prose to the uncanny experiences that have shaped his life while living in the United States - and the charming, bohemian-esque friends he interacts with on a daily basis.
